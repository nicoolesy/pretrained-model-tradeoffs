§ 02Reading the Trade-Offs

You can't optimize all three at once

Size, accuracy, and speed pull against each other. The single most important caveat: accuracy is not one number across domains — a 175B language model and a 3.4M vision model are measured on entirely different benchmarks, so the matrix compares them within domain, not across it.

Axis 01 — Size

Parameters cost money

The param span here is roughly 51,000× — from MobileNetV2's 3.4M to GPT-3's 175B. More parameters generally buy capability, but they also raise memory, latency, and serving cost. Tabular ensembles sidestep the param race entirely.

Axis 02 — Accuracy

Benchmarks don't transfer

ImageNet top-1, GLUE, and tabular AUC live in separate worlds. EfficientNet-B7's 84.3% says nothing about GPT-3's usefulness. Always read accuracy against the benchmark that matches the task — never as a universal score.

Axis 03 — Speed

Speed is a deployment choice

MobileNetV2 and LightGBM are fast because they were designed for constrained settings. Latency is hardware- and batch-dependent, which is why every speed rating here is flagged as characterized, not measured.

The fourth axis — interpretability — is where the domains separate most sharply. Tree ensembles can explain a prediction with feature importance and SHAP values; deep networks largely cannot. When a model's output must be justified to a regulator or a stakeholder, that gap often outweighs a few accuracy points.
§ 03Recommendations by Domain

What to pick, and when

NLP / GenAI
GPT-3 for generation · BERT-base for understanding

Reach for a generative model only when you actually need open-ended text. For classification, search, or embeddings, BERT-base delivers most of the value at a fraction of the size and is far cheaper to fine-tune and serve.

Vision
MobileNetV2 on the edge · EfficientNet-B7 on the server

The choice is purely deployment context. If it runs on a phone or in real time, the 3.4M-param model wins. If it runs in a data center and accuracy is the priority, spend the extra parameters on B7's 84.3%.

Tabular
LightGBM to move fast · XGBoost as the proven default

For structured data, gradient-boosted trees usually beat deep learning while staying explainable. LightGBM trains faster on large data; XGBoost is the battle-tested baseline. Either gives you SHAP-based explanations out of the box.

§ 04Method & Sources

How these figures were gathered

Models were chosen to span all three domains the assignment names, with at least two per domain to make within-domain comparison meaningful. Parameter counts and ImageNet top-1 accuracy are taken directly from each model's original publication. Speed, interpretability, and tabular accuracy are qualitative characterizations drawn from those same sources and standard practice — they are flagged distinctly because they have no single comparable benchmark value.

  • Brown, T. B., Mann, B., Ryder, N., Subbiah, M., Kaplan, J., Dhariwal, P., … Amodei, D. (2020). Language models are few-shot learners [GPT-3]. arXiv:2005.14165.
